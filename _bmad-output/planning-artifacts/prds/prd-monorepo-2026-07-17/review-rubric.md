# PRD Quality Review — prd-monorepo-2026-07-17

## Overall verdict
This PRD is unusually disciplined for its stakes: every FR carries testable consequences, every inference is `[ASSUMPTION]`-tagged and indexed with a clean roundtrip, Success Metrics validate the stated thesis rather than measuring activity, and counter-metrics guard against gaming them. The main risks are minor — a hedge in FR-4 ("si existe") that leaves one consequence ambiguous, and a handful of FRs (2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8) with no Success Metric tracing back to them. Nothing here is broken; the fixes are small, targeted edits, not structural rework.

## Decision-readiness — strong
The PRD states real decisions rather than burying them as considerations: Native Federation over Module Federation (§4.1, elaborated in `addendum.md`), and the MVP1/MVP2 split with MVP2's shape already fixed (dynamic federation via Manifest, §6.3) while its FRs and success criteria are explicitly left undefined. The Open Questions in §12 are genuinely open — none is a rhetorical question answered in the next line (e.g., OQ3 on hosting provider lists four candidates with no lean stated; OQ4 on whether automated testing is even in scope is left fully unresolved). The `[NOTE FOR PM]` in §6.2 lands on a real tension: deferring per-Remote CI/CD is flagged as "precisamente lo que demuestra 'independent deployability' de verdad" — an honest admission that the MVP's FR-9 (isolated serve/build) is a weaker proxy for the architectural promise the project is actually testing. The addendum reinforces this by explicitly naming the gap between "independent deployability" and "build-time coupling via Nx" (addendum, "Pitfalls comunes," 4th bullet) rather than letting the PRD's language paper over it.

### Findings
- **low** Native Federation downside not named (§4.1 / `addendum.md`) — the addendum documents *why* Native Federation was chosen and *when* Module Federation would still be correct, but never states what is given up by choosing the younger, less battle-tested tooling (smaller ecosystem, fewer production war-stories than webpack Module Federation). *Fix:* add one sentence to the addendum's "Por qué" section naming this cost, even briefly.

## Substance over theater — strong
No findings of consequence. The JTBD framing (§2.1) is three angles on one real person (Antonio), not inflated multi-persona theater — and §2.2 explicitly forecloses the reader's temptation to treat the portfolio visitor as a second full persona ("no crea cuenta real ni transacciona"). NFRs in §7 are product-specific and bounded (build <10s, no version-mismatch errors, `nx affected` correctness) rather than the generic "must be scalable/secure/reliable" boilerplate the rubric warns about. The Vision (§1) names the exact stack, the exact three MFEs, and explicitly disclaims building "una tienda real" — it could not be swapped into another PRD unchanged.

## Strategic coherence — strong
The thesis is stated plainly in §1 and §2.1: learn microfrontend coordination hands-on (not from an article) and produce portfolio-credible evidence of it. The roadmap (§10) follows that thesis rather than raw ease — Fase 0 establishes federation + isolated build/serve (the two things the thesis is actually about) before any product feature exists, and cross-remote state/session (Fase 2) is sequenced after the design system exists to consume it. Success Metrics avoid the vanity-metric trap: SM-1 measures whether Antonio can *explain* the mechanism, not whether code exists; SM-2 measures the specific architectural risk (version mismatch) the whole project is built to surface. Counter-metrics SM-C1 and SM-C2 explicitly counterweight the primary metrics against exactly the failure modes a solo learner is prone to — copying tutorials without understanding (counters SM-3) and faking independence via shortcuts (counters SM-1/SM-2). This pairing is rare and does real work here.

## Done-ness clarity — strong
Every FR (FR-1 through FR-11) carries a "Consequences (testable)" block with concrete, checkable conditions — bundle counts (FR-3: "al menos 8-12 productos... al menos 3 categorías"), DevTools-observable behavior (FR-1), and F5-reload persistence checks (FR-6). NFRs in §7 are bounded, not adjectival (build <10s, bundle <300KB per §9, singleton-only version resolution).

### Findings
- **medium** Conditional requirement inside a testable consequence (§4.2, FR-4) — "El contador de items en el header del Shell (**si existe**) se actualiza sin recarga de página al agregar un producto" leaves it undecided whether a header cart-counter is in scope at all. As written, this consequence can never fail: if the counter doesn't exist, the condition is vacuously satisfied. An engineer building FR-4 has no way to know if a header counter is required. *Fix:* either commit to the counter as part of FR-2/FR-4 scope (and make it a hard consequence) or drop the clause and note it as a stretch/nice-to-have in §6.2.
- **low** Subjective term in a consequence (§4.1, FR-2) — "El header no se recarga ni **parpadea**" ("flickers") mixes one testable condition (no full reload) with one perceptual one (no flicker) that has no defined threshold. *Fix:* either drop "parpadea" or tie it to something observable (e.g., no layout-shift/CLS spike, or same DOM node persists across navigation).

## Scope honesty — strong
§5 (Non-Goals) and §6.2 (Out of Scope for MVP) are cleanly separated — permanent exclusions (real payments, real backend, polyglot federation, real auth, i18n) versus MVP-deferred items with reasoning (per-Remote CI/CD, dynamic federation, cross-remote e2e tests, cart persistence). §6.3 goes further than most PRDs at this scale by naming that MVP2's *architecture* is already decided (dynamic federation via Manifest) while its *scope* is explicitly not — an honest, unforced admission rather than silent scope creep bait. All seven inline `[ASSUMPTION]` tags (§4.2, §4.5, §4.8, §8, §9 ×2, §10) roundtrip cleanly into the §13 Assumptions Index — no orphaned inline tags, no unindexed assumptions found. Open-items density (5 Open Questions + 7 Assumptions + 1 NOTE FOR PM ≈ 13) is proportionate to the "proyecto interno serio" stakes the user set — this is not a green-light-to-build document, and the density reads as honest hedging rather than avoidance.

## Downstream usability — strong
The Glossary (§3) is used consistently — "Remote" and "Microfrontend/MFE" are declared synonyms and used interchangeably without drift; "Federación estática/dinámica," "Singleton," and "Affected graph" all appear in FRs/NFRs exactly as defined. FR IDs (FR-1–FR-11), UJ IDs (UJ-1, UJ-2), and SM IDs (SM-1–SM-5, SM-C1–SM-C2) are contiguous with no gaps or duplicates. Every FR is claimed by exactly one phase in the §10 Roadmap (cross-checked: FR-1/FR-9→Fase 0, FR-3/FR-8→Fase 1, FR-4/FR-5/FR-7→Fase 2, FR-2/FR-6→Fase 3, FR-10/FR-11→Fase 4 — all eleven accounted for, none orphaned). This PRD explicitly feeds downstream architecture/UX work (the addendum states it is "insumo directo para bmad-architecture / bmad-ux"), so this dimension carries real weight, and it holds up.

### Findings
- **low** Partial FR→SM traceability (§11 vs §4) — SM-1, SM-2, SM-3 "Validate" FR-1, FR-7, FR-9, FR-10, FR-11, but FR-2, FR-3, FR-4, FR-5, FR-6, and FR-8 are never named as validated by any Success Metric (only reachable via the Roadmap's "Realiza" links). For a solo-operator learning project this is a minor gap, not a blocker, but a downstream reader mapping SMs to FRs for a traceability matrix will hit six FRs with no SM anchor. *Fix:* either fold these FRs under SM-2/SM-3's existing validation language ("Shell + 3 Remotes corren federados... end-to-end") or accept explicitly that not every FR needs a dedicated SM given the project's thesis is architectural, not feature-complete-ness.

## Shape fit — strong
This is correctly shaped as a single-operator technical capability spec with a dual (learning + portfolio) purpose, not forced into a consumer-product mold. §2.3 self-calibrates UJ treatment explicitly ("Alcance ligero... una frase por journey es suficiente") and delivers exactly that — two one-line UJs, each with a named protagonist (Antonio; "un visitante del portafolio"), no floating UJs. §2.2's Non-Users section does real work by foreclosing invented stakeholder complexity the project doesn't have. Success Metrics are appropriately operational/self-assessed (SM-1: "Antonio puede explicar y demostrar en voz alta") rather than forced into user-facing engagement metrics that wouldn't fit a project with no real users. No enterprise apparatus (stakeholder RACI, competitive analysis, market sizing) was bolted on, and no UJ-density inflation was used to simulate thoroughness. The addendum/PRD split itself is a good shape decision — implementation-level research and pitfalls live separately from the capability decisions the PRD body needs, keeping the PRD lean while preserving the research trail for the architecture phase.
